PRE-CALL (2 minutes before)
Pull up their application. Check five things:


1. Age — adjusts your language and which student stories you use
2. Available capital — tells you which tier to target and which money objection is coming
3. What's stopping them (Q5) — this is the objection you'll face. Prepare your response now.
4. Strategy interest — BRRRR, wholesaling, ADU, buy-and-hold. Tailor your examples.
5. How they found us — webinar, challenge ($27), paid ad, organic. Warmer sources need less discovery.


If they came through the 5-Day Challenge, they already paid money, showed up for 5 days, and self-selected. Move faster through discovery. If they came through a cold ad, they watched a 2-minute video of Thach and clicked a button. They need more warming.

PHASE 1: OPENING (2 minutes)
"[Name]! Hey, it's [Your Name] from Springboard To Wealth, calling on a recorded line — how's it going?"


Wait. Match their energy. If they're upbeat, be upbeat. If they're calm, be calm.


"Appreciate you taking the time. I know you're busy so I want to be respectful of your schedule. We've got about 30 minutes together. Cool if we jump right in?"


Set the decision frame early (this is critical — it prevents "let me think about it" later):


"Before we get into anything, I want to set expectations. By the end of this call, you'll know exactly what the program is, what it costs, and whether it's right for you. All I ask is that you give me an honest answer — yes or no. The only answers we try to avoid are the ones in between, like 'let me think about it' or 'I need to check with someone.' Those usually just mean we didn't do a good job explaining something. Fair enough?"


Wait for agreement. They just committed to giving you a decision.


Disclaimer (builds trust, not a legal hedge):


"One more thing — we don't make any promises or guarantees of specific results. Your results depend on your effort. I'd rather be honest with you upfront than overpromise. Fair?"


"Great. So tell me — what caught your eye about Springboard To Wealth? What made you book this call?"


Shut up. Let them talk. Their answer tells you what hook brought them in and what they're hoping for.

PHASE 2: DISCOVERY (6-8 minutes)
Your only job in this phase is to understand three things: where they are, where they want to be, and what's been keeping them stuck. Do not pitch. Do not mention the program. Ask questions and listen.


Their talk ratio should be higher than yours in this phase. If you're talking more than 40%, you're talking too much.
Current state
"Tell me about your situation right now. What does your day-to-day look like — work, finances, all of it?"


Listen. Take notes. You'll use their exact words back to them later.
Desired state
"Where do you want to be 12 months from now? If everything went right, what does your life actually look like?"


Push for specifics. "Financial freedom" is not an answer. Make them paint the picture.


"What does financial freedom mean to you specifically? Is that a number? A lifestyle? A date?"


If they say "$10K/month passive income" — that's gold. You now have a target to tie the offer to.
Emotional anchor (the Hormozi "why behind the why")
"Why is that so important to you?"


This is where the real motivation lives. They'll say something about their kids, their spouse, their parents, their legacy, their freedom. Whatever they say — write it down word for word. You're going to use it at the close.


"And what happens if you DON'T hit that goal? If 12 months from now, nothing's changed?"


Let them sit with the discomfort. Do not fill the silence.
Past attempts
"What have you tried so far? Courses, books, YouTube, meetups — anything?"
What stopped them
"What do you think held you back from making it happen?"


Their answer here maps to what they wrote on the application form. If it matches, the objection is genuine. If it's different, dig deeper — the real blocker might be something they didn't want to write down.
Decision-maker check
"Is anyone else involved in this decision — spouse, partner, business partner?"


If yes:


"Are they on the same page about wanting to invest in real estate? Would they be supportive if you found the right program?"


This prevents the "I need to talk to my spouse" objection at the close. If the spouse isn't on board, you need to know now.
Urgency
"Why now? What made you say 'today's the day I'm going to look into this'?"

PHASE 3: DIAGNOSIS (3-4 minutes)
Reflect back everything they told you. Use their exact words. Then widen the gap between where they are and where they want to be.


"Let me make sure I've got this right. You're [current situation — use their words]. You've tried [past attempts]. What you really want is [desired outcome — their words]. And the thing that's been missing is [gap they identified]. Sound right?"


Wait for "yes."


"Can I be straight with you for a second — not as a salesperson, but as someone who talks to people in your exact position every single day?"


Wait for permission.
Agitate the cost of inaction
"Here's what I see happen to people in your situation who don't get the right guidance. They spend the next 12-18 months 'researching.' They watch 200 YouTube videos. They read 3 books. They go to a meetup or two. And at the end of all that — they still haven't bought a single property. Not because they're not smart enough. Because nobody told them which deal to buy, how to run the numbers, or when to pull the trigger."


"And the ones who DO jump in without guidance? They buy the wrong property. They overpay by $30-50K because they didn't know how to comp it. They hire the wrong contractor and blow $15-25K on a bad rehab. They pick the wrong financing and pay $300-500 extra per month for YEARS. One bad deal can cost $50-100K. I've seen it happen hundreds of times."


"That's the real cost of figuring this out alone. It's not $8K. It's $50-100K in preventable mistakes plus 12-18 months of your life you never get back."


Pause. Let that land.


"Now flip it. What if 12 months from now you've closed your first deal? You're getting rental income. You've got equity building. You know exactly what to do for deal #2. How does that feel?"


The contrast between pain (doing nothing) and pleasure (taking action) is what creates urgency to decide today.

PHASE 6: THE CLOSING LOOP (3-5 minutes)
This is where most closers lose deals. They hear hesitation and back off. You don't back off. You lean in with curiosity.


You will ask for the sale at least 3-4 times. Most deals close on the 3rd or 4th ask. If you only ask once, you're leaving money on every call.
Step 1: Confirm the program can help
"I hear you. Do you mind if I ask you something?"


"Setting price aside for a second — do you believe a program like Springboard To Wealth could help you get to [their goal from discovery]?"


Three possible responses:


If "Yes, but [objection]":


"Got it — so the main thing is [objection]. Other than that, is there anything else holding you back?"


If they name something else, handle THAT first (it's usually the real one). If "no, just that" — you've isolated the objection. Handle it using the objection responses below, then ask again.


If uncertain / "yeah, I guess...":


"You don't sound super confident. What's your main concern?"


Get to the real objection. Then isolate: "Other than that, anything else?"


If "Yes, I do" (confident):


"I agree. Given everything you told me — [mirror their situation back] — this is built for you. So what's stopping us from getting you started right now?"


Whatever they say, isolate it: "Other than [that], anything else?"
Step 2: Handle the objection
Use the specific responses below. Match the objection to the response.
Step 3: Ask for the sale again
"So it sounds like we've addressed [objection]. Ready to get started?"
Step 4: If a new objection surfaces — repeat the loop
When someone switches objections, you're making progress. They're peeling layers. The first objection is almost never the real one. Keep going.
The Isolation Loop (model this — it's the #1 closer technique from transcript analysis)
"If we can get [their objection] handled, is there anything else that would stop you from moving forward today?"


Repeat until they say "No, nothing else." At that point, they've psychologically committed. Handle the one remaining objection and close.

PHASE 8: CLOSE LOST — BAMFAM (1-2 minutes)
You've run the loops 3-4 times and can't close today. Do NOT let them leave without a booked next meeting.


"I can see we're running short on time and I want to respect your schedule. We've covered a lot of ground today. I genuinely believe this could change your situation based on everything you told me. When can we pick this back up? I have [Tuesday at 2pm or Thursday at 11am] — which works better?"


Wait. Book it on the spot.


"Perfect. When we talk next, I'll have [something specific ready for them — a deal analysis example in their market, a student success story that matches their situation, answers to the questions they raised]. You'll get a calendar invite in the next 60 seconds."


NEVER end a call with:


* "I'll send you some info" — that's where deals go to die
* "Think about it and get back to me" — they won't
* "Call me when you're ready" — you just lost a customer
* "No pressure" / "It's up to you" — removes all urgency


Every call ends with either a sale or a booked next meeting. No exceptions.

THE VALUE EQUATION — KEEP THIS IN YOUR HEAD
                Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood


Value  =  ────────────────────────────────────────────────


                Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice


Every word you say on the call should move one of these four levers:


* Maximize Dream Outcome — Make it vivid. Not "build wealth." Instead: "You wake up Tuesday, check your phone, see $8,400 deposited from 10 rental properties. You go golfing. You pick up your kids at 3pm."
* Maximize Perceived Likelihood — Stack proof. "4,000+ students. Thong and Shanny went from zero to $900K equity. Ian Love: $4M equity, 12 properties in 2 years."
* Minimize Time Delay — "Most students find their first deal within 3-6 months. By month 12, they've closed it."
* Minimize Effort & Sacrifice — "You don't quit your job. You attend coaching calls twice a week, analyze one deal a week, and follow the system. An hour a day."


When all four levers are working, the prospect does the math in their head and the only rational answer is yes. The offer is so good they feel stupid saying no. That's the goal of every single call.
